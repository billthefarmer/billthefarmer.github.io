Slowing Down Video

Tue Sep 7, 2021 by Bill Farmer. Categories: Hacking .

Twenty years ago, when we joined a morris side and started learning to play melodeon there was only one free app for slowing down audio that I could find and it was horrible. If you tried to slow down more than a bit it went all echoey and unrecognisable.

There’s a tune, Brother Trip Foot - A Traditional Tune Played on Melodeon, played by Mcgrooger, part of Melnet TotM for September that we both love. But it’s difficult to learn, and we like the cross fingering.

I use FFMpeg and FFPlay but have never tried to slow anything down. So I looked it up, as you do. The command line for half speed is

$ ffmpeg -i Brother\ Trip\ Foot\ -\ A\ Traditional\ Tune\ Played\ on\ Melodeon.mp4 \
  -filter_complex "[0:v]setpts=2.0*PTS[v];[0:a]atempo=0.5[a]" -map "[v]" \
  -map "[a]" half.mp4

No, I don’t understand it either. But the result is wonderful. So listen and watch at half speed. I truncated it at 1:25 because the original file is over 100Mb.
